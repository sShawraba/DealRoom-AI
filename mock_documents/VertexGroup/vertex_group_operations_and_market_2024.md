## Market Context

### UK Specialty Fashion Retail — Structural Decline

The UK specialty fashion retail sector has experienced sustained structural headwinds since 2016, accelerated by COVID-19 and the cost-of-living crisis. Total sector revenue declined from GBP 58B in 2019 to GBP 49B in 2023, a contraction of 15.5% in real terms. Key drivers include:

- Online penetration rising from 19% (2019) to 36% (2024), largely captured by pure-play and vertically integrated brands rather than traditional store-based retailers
- Value polarisation — consumer spending concentrating at the extreme value end (Primark, Shein, Temu) and premium/accessible-luxury end (ZARA, M&S Clothing, & Other Stories), compressing mid-market players
- Cost-of-living crisis (2022-2024) causing discretionary clothing spend to fall 8.3% in real terms
- High street footfall 22% below 2019 levels (Springboard, 2024); shopping centre footfall 17% below 2019

Vertex is positioned in the mid-market segment — the most structurally challenged part of the market. Direct comparators Debenhams (administration 2021), M&Co (administration 2022), Joules (administration 2022), and Wilko (administration 2023) all failed in this environment.

### Loss-Making Store Detail

Management and A&M have identified 127 stores as structurally loss-making on a four-wall EBITDA basis (i.e., store-level revenue minus directly attributable cost of goods, store labour, occupancy, and store depreciation). Of these:

- 68 stores: negative four-wall EBITDA even before lease cost
- 59 stores: positive four-wall EBITDA before lease but negative after lease cost
- Average loss per loss-making store: GBP 214K per annum
- Total annual loss from 127 stores: GBP 27.2M

The 86 stores with leases expiring within 2 years represent a natural de-risking opportunity — management can exit these locations without CVA or landlord negotiation. However, 41 of these are among the Company's better-performing stores and would represent a revenue reduction of approximately GBP 98M if not renewed.

## Omnichannel Transformation — Project Nova (Post-Mortem)

### Programme Overview

Project Nova was a GBP 79M omnichannel transformation programme initiated in FY2022, intended to:
1. Replace the legacy IBM WebSphere e-commerce platform with Salesforce Commerce Cloud
2. Implement a single unified customer data platform (CDP) across all channels
3. Launch same-day delivery and in-store fulfilment ("Click & Collect 2.0")
4. Migrate to a new ERP (SAP S/4HANA) from legacy SAP ECC6

## Due Diligence Conclusion

Vertex Group plc presents extreme financial distress with a high probability of entering formal insolvency proceedings within 8-12 weeks absent a successful restructuring. The deal team should consider three primary scenarios:

**Scenario A — Pre-Pack Administration (probability: 45%):** An acquirer purchases the business and selected assets from an administrator, leaving legacy debt, leases, and pension deficit with the estate. Estimated secured creditor recovery: 28-42p in the pound.

**Scenario B — Consensual Debt-for-Equity (probability: 40%):** Lender group converts GBP 400M of TLB to equity, extinguishing Meridian's stake. Requires unanimous lender consent; Apollo's adversarial stance is the primary execution risk.

**Scenario C — CVA-led wind-down (probability: 15%):** CVA to exit 89 loss-making stores while trading from 223 sites. Requires 75% creditor vote; landlord opposition makes this unlikely.

**This transaction is rated CRITICAL RISK.** Any investment decision requires enhanced Investment Committee governance approval and parallel engagement of insolvency counsel.
