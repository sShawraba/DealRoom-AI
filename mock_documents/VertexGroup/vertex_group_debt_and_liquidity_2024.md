## Overview

Vertex Group plc carries GBP 680M in drawn senior secured debt (Term Loan B: GBP 620M; RCF: GBP 60M) originated at the time of the October 2019 leveraged buyout by Meridian Capital Partners. The debt was structured at a 5.1x entry leverage ratio against FY2019 pro-forma EBITDA of GBP 137M. Cumulative EBITDA deterioration since inception has rendered the capital structure irrecoverably overleveraged. Both senior facilities are in event of default and subject to a conditional standstill agreement expiring 30 September 2024.

## Covenant Breach & Standstill Agreement

### Breach Detail

The senior facilities agreement requires the Company to maintain Net Leverage below 6.5x Net Debt / EBITDA, tested semi-annually at 30 September and 31 March. As of the September 2023 and March 2024 test dates, the Company reported negative EBITDA, rendering the leverage ratio incalculable and constituting an automatic event of default under clause 24.1(b) of the facilities agreement.

A financial maintenance covenant waiver request was submitted to the agent bank (Barclays) in August 2023. The lender group declined to grant a permanent waiver but agreed a conditional standstill.

### Standstill Conditions (Conditional Waiver Agreement, February 2024)

The lender group granted a conditional standstill valid through 30 September 2024 on the following conditions:

1. Appointment of Alvarez & Marsal as Chief Restructuring Officer — completed 14 March 2024
2. Weekly cash reporting to the agent bank commencing 21 March 2024
3. No additional indebtedness without unanimous lender consent
4. No disposal of assets exceeding GBP 2M without lender consent
5. Delivery of a lender-approved restructuring plan no later than 31 July 2024

Failure to deliver the restructuring plan within the standstill window will trigger an acceleration event, at which point the GBP 620M TLB and GBP 60M RCF become immediately repayable.

## Restructuring Scenarios & Creditor Recovery Analysis

### Scenario A: Consensual Debt-for-Equity Swap

The Company and A&M are proposing a GBP 400M debt-for-equity conversion across the TLB, wiping out Meridian Capital Partners' equity stake and transferring ownership to the lender syndicate. The remaining GBP 220M TLB would be restructured at SONIA + 300bps with a 3-year extension to October 2029.

| Stakeholder | Pre-Restructuring | Post-Restructuring |
|---|---|---|
| Meridian Capital Partners (equity) | -GBP 327M (negative equity) | Zero — full wipeout |
| TLB Lender Syndicate | GBP 620M face value debt | 100% equity + GBP 220M restructured debt |
| RCF Lenders | GBP 60M drawn | Rolled into restructured facility at par |
| Trade Creditors | ~GBP 211M payables | Paid under CVA at 75-85p in the pound |

Probability assessment: 40%. Requires unanimous lender consent. Apollo's adversarial stance is the key risk.

### Scenario B: Pre-Pack Administration

A pre-packaged administration would allow a buyer (trade or financial) to acquire the business and selected assets from an administrator, leaving legacy debt, leases, and pension liabilities with the estate.

Indicative recoveries under administration:
- Secured creditors (TLB/RCF): 28-42p in the pound
- Trade creditors: 3-8p in the pound
- Landlords: Lease surrender; nil cash recovery
- Equity: Zero

Probability assessment: 45%. Most likely outcome if consensual restructuring fails.

### Scenario C: Controlled Wind-Down via CVA

A Company Voluntary Arrangement (CVA) would allow the Company to exit or re-gear leases on up to 89 loss-making stores while continuing to trade from the remaining 223 locations. Requires 75% creditor approval by value.

Estimated annual cash savings if CVA approved: GBP 34M
Required exit costs (landlord claims, redundancy): GBP 47M one-time

Probability assessment: 15%. Landlord community has indicated strong opposition; CBRE estimates CVA vote will fall short of 75% threshold based on preliminary soundings.

## HMRC & Tax Position

The Company has GBP 14.2M of deferred VAT under an HMRC Time to Pay (TTP) arrangement agreed in November 2023, repayable in 18 monthly instalments of approximately GBP 790K. Any insolvency event (administration, CVA) will trigger immediate crystallisation of the full TTP balance. HMRC holds preferential creditor status for post-April 2020 VAT arrears under the Finance Act 2020, ranking ahead of unsecured creditors but behind the secured lender syndicate.

Corporation tax losses carried forward are estimated at GBP 342M. These losses would be extinguished in an administration scenario and have nil value to a buyer in a pre-pack unless specifically structured via a Business Transfer Agreement.
