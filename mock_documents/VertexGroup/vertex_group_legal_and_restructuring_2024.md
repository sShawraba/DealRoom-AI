## Corporate Structure

Vertex Group plc is incorporated in England and Wales (Companies House no. 08841293), listed on AIM prior to the 2019 take-private. The corporate group comprises 14 legal entities across 3 jurisdictions:

- Vertex Group plc (UK holdco — listed shell, now delisted)
- Vertex Retail Limited (UK operating company — principal trading entity)
- Vertex Ireland DAC (Ireland — 24 stores)
- Vertex Netherlands BV (Netherlands — incorporated; trading suspended pending restructuring)
- Vertex Finance Limited (UK — intercompany financing vehicle)
- 9 dormant subsidiaries (UK)

The group employs 6,847 full-time equivalent employees (down from 11,200 at LBO) and approximately 3,100 part-time and seasonal staff. The CFO (Claire Whitmore) resigned in April 2024; an interim CFO (Mark Dalloway, previously Arcadia Group) was appointed in May 2024. The CEO (Jonathan Reeve) has been in post since January 2023 following the departure of the LBO-era CEO.

## Intellectual Property

Vertex holds the following material IP assets:

- "Vertex" brand trademark — registered in UK, EU, Australia, Canada (Class 25: clothing, footwear, headgear)
- "VX" device mark — registered UK and EU
- 3 design registrations for proprietary knitwear patterns
- vertex.co.uk and vertex.com domain names

Brand impairment of GBP 168M was recognised in FY2024, reducing the carrying value of brand intangibles from GBP 312M to GBP 144M. The brand retains residual licensing value estimated at GBP 35M–55M by CBRE Brand Finance (commissioned March 2024), primarily driven by the "Vertex Heritage" archival range and loyal 45-65 age demographic customer base.

The IP portfolio is unencumbered and available as a standalone disposal asset. A trade sale of the brand and IP alone — without the store estate — has been explored with three prospective licensee buyers, with indicative interest at GBP 28M–40M.

## Employment & Pension

### Workforce

The Company has reduced its headcount by 36% since the LBO (from 11,200 to approximately 7,200 FTE + part-time). A further redundancy programme of approximately 800 roles was announced in Q1 FY2024 but has been paused pending the outcome of the restructuring process, as execution would trigger a GBP 12M redundancy liability.

All employees transferred automatically under TUPE protections from the LBO structure in 2019. Any pre-pack administration buyer would be subject to TUPE obligations for employees associated with the acquired business.

### Pension

Vertex operates a closed defined benefit pension scheme (the Vertex Retirement Plan) with 2,847 deferred members and 1,104 current pensioners. As at the most recent actuarial valuation (March 2023), the scheme had:

- Liabilities: GBP 218M
- Assets: GBP 177M
- Deficit: GBP 41M (funding basis)

The insolvency of the sponsoring employer would constitute an "assessment event" under section 120 of the Pensions Act 2004, triggering entry into the Pension Protection Fund. PPF compensation pays 90% of accrued benefits (capped at GBP 41,461 per year for early leavers). The PPF has been formally notified and a PPF monitoring relationship established.

## CVA — Legal Framework & Landlord Position

A Company Voluntary Arrangement (CVA) under Part I of the Insolvency Act 1986 would allow the Company to modify lease obligations without landlord consent, provided the CVA achieves 75% creditor approval by value. CVAs are the primary restructuring tool used in UK retail distress (precedents: New Look 2020, Monsoon 2020, Caffe Nero 2020).

### Preliminary Landlord Soundings

CBRE Real Estate Advisory has conducted preliminary outreach to landlords representing 89 loss-making stores targeted for closure or rent reduction.

| Landlord | Stores Affected | Preliminary Stance |
|---|---|---|
| British Land | 11 | Opposed; threatening injunction |
| Hammerson | 8 | Constructive; willing to discuss re-gears |
| Landsec | 14 | Opposed |
| NewRiver REIT | 7 | Constructive |
| Aberdeen Standard (now abrdn) | 6 | Neutral |
| Local authority landlords | 18 | Constructive |
| Private landlords | 25 | Mixed |

CBRE estimates that securing 75% creditor approval by value is unlikely without British Land and Landsec support. Both have publicly opposed recent retail CVAs (New Look, Wilko) and are expected to mount a legal challenge. Counsel opinion (Linklaters, dated May 2024) rates CVA success probability at 15–25%.
